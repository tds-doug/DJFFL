Fantasy Football League History

2002 DJ decides to create fantasy football league on Yahoo! Fantasy Sports.  Original league managers are Doug Johnson (Death To Armenia), Cortney Johnson (Prime Time Playazz), Shay Patel (East Bay Big Tymers), Jason Keledjian (Armenia), Mark McGlaughlin (Sparticus), Logan Wallis (Catbox Worms), Victor Chang (Raiders Empire), and Brian Caynan (Niner Knights).
   
2002 Death To Armenia defeats East Bay Big Tymers in FFL Bowl I (final score lost to history).
   
2003 Due to the success of the first season, DJ continues the FFL on Yahoo!.  Brian Caynan and Victor Chang decide not to participate.  Jake Garris (NorCal Nuggets) joins the league.  East Bay Big Tymers changes team name to East Bay Bottle Rockets.  Catbox Worms changes team name to Team Kill.  Sparticus changes team name to Maximus Sparticus.  Prime Time Playaz changes team name to Here Comes Da Pain.
   
2003 Armenia defeats Death To Armenia in FFL Bowl II.
   
2004 Due to limitations with the Yahoo! Fantasy Sports leagues, DJ moves the league to his home server and does major overhaul of league rules.
   
2004 Paul Etcheverry (The Maverick Boilermakers) joins the league.
   
2004 Armenia defeats Here Comes Da Pain in FFL Bowl III.
