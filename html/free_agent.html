Free Agent Market

Any player who was active the previous year and is not under a contract will be considered a free agent.

Before the draft the free agent market will occur.

During the free agent market any manager can bid on a free agent. A bid must be at least equal to the player's current value.

If the free agent was previously contracted the manager who owned the contract may match the highest bid. The manager who made the high bid may then bid again to try to win the free agent.

A free agent who is purchased during the free agent market will be considered to have been signed to a contract. The duration of the contract is up to the discretion of manager who now owns him.

If a free agent is purchased during the free agent market and is later dropped before the end of the free agent market or during the draft, it will be treated the same as if the free agent had been placed on waivers during the last year of a contract. This means that the manager placing the free agent on waivers will have to pay a $2 salary cap penalty.
