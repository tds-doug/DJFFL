Contracts

A contract is defined as an agreement between a player and a manager to play for a pre-determined amount of money for more then 1 year.

Contracts shall be for 1, 2 or 3 years. A 1 year contract lasts through the end of the current season.

Contracts signed before the start of a season will be at the upcoming seasons value. Any contract signed after the start of a season will be at the next season's value.

A contracted player may be placed on waivers at a penalty of $2 per year remaining on contract for each remaining year. If you drop a contracted player in the first year of a three contract you will pay a penalty of $6 the first year, $4 the next, and $2 the last.

If someone picks up a contracted player from waivers the manager owning the contract will still pay the penalty for that year, but the following year will not have to pay any remaining penalties.

A manager picking up a previously contracted player from waivers pays him the current market value, not the previous contract.

A manager can have as many contracted players as he can afford. He cannot sign any player to a contract if it puts him over the cap.

If a player is worth more then his contracted value for two consecutive seasons he will be considered "unhappy" and will not honor his contract for the remaining time. The manager will have to "make a deal" with the player and resign the player for the remainder of the contract at previous season or current seasons value (up to the discretion of the owner).

A manager can move any player at any time to the Injured Reserve list. This player is ineligible to play the remainder of the season, but relinquishes 1/2 of his salary for that year. This allows the manager to pick up some backup players for the remainder of the season.

If a player retires, dies, suffers a career ending injury, or is convicted of a felony (and expelled from the league) the manager does not have to honor the contract and does not suffer any penalty. A player released by his team and not picked up before the season opener is considered to have retired. A player release by his team and picked up before the season opener is still bound to his contract despite the team change. If a player retires and then comes out of retirement his previous contract is voided and he is valued at his current value.
